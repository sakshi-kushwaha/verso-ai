TechVenture Inc. — Q4 2025 Earnings Report

Executive Summary: TechVenture closed Q4 2025 with record revenue of $187.4M, a 22% increase year-over-year and 8% above consensus estimates. This performance was driven by exceptional growth in our Enterprise AI Platform segment, which now accounts for 61% of total revenue, up from 48% in Q4 2024. The quarter marks our sixth consecutive period of accelerating growth, validating our strategic decision to pivot from consulting-led services to a product-led growth model beginning in 2023. We achieved GAAP profitability for the third consecutive quarter, a milestone that reflects both strong revenue scaling and disciplined cost management across the organization.

Revenue Breakdown: Enterprise AI Platform revenue reached $114.3M (+41% YoY), fueled by 47 new enterprise contracts with average deal sizes exceeding $500K ARR. Notably, 12 of these contracts exceeded $1M ARR, compared to just 4 deals of that size in Q4 2024. The platform's model-serving infrastructure and built-in governance features continue to be the primary differentiators in competitive evaluations, with a win rate of 68% in head-to-head comparisons against the three leading competitors. Our mid-market SaaS segment contributed $52.8M (+12% YoY), with net revenue retention improving to 118% from 112% in the prior quarter. This improvement was driven by the launch of our Advanced Analytics add-on module in October, which has already been adopted by 23% of existing mid-market customers. The average revenue per mid-market account increased to $4,200/month from $3,600/month. Legacy consulting services declined to $20.3M (-15% YoY) as planned, reflecting our strategic pivot toward product-led growth. We expect this segment to stabilize at approximately $18M/quarter as we retain high-value advisory engagements while sunsetting lower-margin implementation projects.

Profitability Metrics: Gross margin expanded to 74.8%, up 320 basis points from Q4 2024, primarily due to improved infrastructure utilization and the shift toward higher-margin AI platform deals. Cloud infrastructure costs per customer decreased 18% as our engineering team optimized model-serving efficiency and negotiated improved rates with our primary cloud provider. Operating margin reached 18.2%, compared to 12.7% in Q4 2024. Operating expenses grew 14% YoY, well below revenue growth of 22%, demonstrating strong operating leverage. Sales and marketing spend as a percentage of revenue declined to 28% from 33%, reflecting improved efficiency in our demand generation programs and growing contribution from organic and partner channels. Adjusted EBITDA was $42.1M (22.5% margin), exceeding our guidance range of $36-39M. Free cash flow was $31.2M, up 67% YoY, with cash conversion improving to 74% of adjusted EBITDA. We ended the quarter with $234M in cash and short-term investments and zero long-term debt.

Customer Metrics: Total paying customers grew to 2,847 (+18% YoY), with enterprise customers (ARR exceeding $100K) reaching 312 (+34% YoY). Gross revenue churn decreased to 4.2% annualized from 5.8% in Q4 2024, reflecting improved product stability and our expanded customer success team. Net Promoter Score improved to 72 from 65, driven by investments in platform reliability (99.97% uptime in Q4), faster support response times (median first response under 2 hours), and the launch of our self-service knowledge base. Customer acquisition cost for enterprise decreased to $12,400, down from $15,800, driven by increasingly efficient inbound marketing, partner referrals (now contributing 31% of enterprise pipeline), and a growing brand reputation in the AI infrastructure space.

Strategic Initiatives: During Q4, we completed the acquisition of NeuralOps for $45M in cash and stock, adding federated learning capabilities to our platform. The NeuralOps team of 28 engineers has been integrated into our platform engineering group, and we expect to launch the federated learning module as a generally available feature in Q2 2026. We opened our first Asia-Pacific office in Singapore with 35 employees across sales, engineering, and customer success functions, and plan to expand to 80 employees by mid-2026. Early traction in the APAC market has been encouraging, with 8 enterprise deals signed in the first 10 weeks of operation. R&D spending increased to 28% of revenue as we accelerated development of our next-generation model serving infrastructure, which will support multi-modal models and real-time fine-tuning capabilities expected to launch in H2 2026.

Guidance for Q1 2026: We expect revenue of $195-202M, representing 20-25% YoY growth. Operating margin is expected to be 16-18% as we absorb NeuralOps integration costs and APAC expansion expenses, both of which we view as high-ROI investments. We are raising our full-year 2026 revenue guidance to $820-850M from $780-810M previously, reflecting the strong Q4 momentum and growing enterprise pipeline entering the new year.
